Below, we will be presenting several visualizations regarding problem solutions

Number of accepted
solutions per level

The first visualization is a Pie chart with the number of accepted solutions for each difficulty level in Codechef.
The result shows, as expected, that the hardest level (challenge) solutions are the fewest in number due to the problems' difficulty and the most accepted solutions are in the easy difficulty. This means that users who are probably new to Competitive Programming do not go to beginner problems as much and instead opt for something harder, to keep both the enjoyability of writing code and simultaneously learning new ways to solve problems without burning their brains out.

Tested vs Untested solutions

Codechef, apart from having a fully automatic pipeline that compiles, runs and tests solutions, invites experienced users to run test cases for problems manually, create solutions and compensates them with a small amount of money for it!
With this visualization, we want to show the importance of Testing. Manual handling of test cases ensures that errors related to other factors than wrong code, such as wrong test case writing, are minimized or completely removed.
For accepted solutions, it is apparent that we have a larger amount of tested problems, which leads to the conclusion that this difference between tested and untested accepted solutions could be poorly written automated test cases, or wrong problem statements.
For programs that ended up with runtime-errors, tested programs are less than untested ones, indicating the advantages of manual testing.

Most difficult problems - Top 25

Problems in codechef have two labels that distinguish them from each other, difficulty and tags.
Tags represent the type of algorithms that are required to solve a problem. The diagram shows the percentage of accepted solutions for each tag.
It is apparent that problems that relate to recursion and prime numbers are the most difficult to solve due to the low percentage of their solutions while problems that relate to sorting, binary search and algebra are easier to solve.
Overall, difficulty in the shown tags is high. The diagram shows that these problems have low percentages of accepted solutions in general, which means they're pretty difficult to solve.
